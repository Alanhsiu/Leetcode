Behavioral prompts cluster into a few themes. Map each of your prepared
[STAR stories](/behavioral/star-method) to one or more of these, and you can handle most rounds.

## Conflict & collaboration
*"Tell me about a disagreement with a coworker."*
Probing: can you disagree without it getting personal, and reach a good outcome? Show that
you sought to understand the other view, used data, and preserved the relationship.

## Failure & ownership
*"Tell me about a time you failed."*
Probing: do you take responsibility and learn? Pick a real failure with real stakes, own
your part without blaming others, and be concrete about what changed afterward.

## Leadership & influence
*"Tell me about a time you led without authority."*
Probing: can you move a group when you're not the boss? Emphasize aligning people on a
goal, communicating, and unblocking others.

## Ambiguity & initiative
*"Tell me about a time you had to act without clear direction."*
Probing: are you self-directed? Show how you scoped the problem, made a call with
incomplete information, and adjusted as you learned.

## Impact & prioritization
*"What's a project you're proud of?" / "How do you decide what to work on?"*
Probing: do you focus on what matters? Tie your work to a measurable outcome and explain
why it was the right thing to do *then*.

## Two questions to always have ready

- **"Why this company / role?"** — a specific, honest reason beats flattery.
- **Your questions for them.** Thoughtful questions about the team and the work signal real
  interest; "no questions" reads as disengaged.

## How to practice

Write the bullet points (not a script) for each story, rehearse aloud, and record yourself
once. You're aiming for *specific and natural*, not memorized.
